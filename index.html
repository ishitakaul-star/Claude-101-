Module 2

How Claude Works

Understanding how to talk to Claude — called "prompting" — is the single biggest factor in getting great results. Let's learn how.

⏱ ~10 minutes
📚 4 topics

The Basics: Prompts & Responses

When you type a message to Claude, that's called a prompt. Claude reads your prompt and generates a response. The quality of Claude's output depends almost entirely on the quality of your prompt.

Think of it like working with a brilliant, extremely capable new colleague. The more context and direction you give them, the better their work will be.

The 4 Elements of a Great Prompt

Element 1

🎯 Role

Tell Claude who to be. "Act as an experienced tax advisor" or "You are a senior software engineer reviewing code."

Element 2

📋 Context

Give background. What project is this for? Who's the audience? What's already been tried? More context = better results.

Element 3

📝 Task

Be specific about what you want. "Write a 3-paragraph summary" is better than "summarize this."

Element 4

📐 Format

Specify the output. Do you want bullet points? A table? A numbered list? A formal tone? Tell Claude.

Prompt Examples: Weak vs. Strong

Here's the same request done poorly vs. done well:

❌ Weak Prompt
Write an email about the product launch.
✅ Strong Prompt
You are a product marketing manager at a fintech company. Write a concise internal announcement email (150–200 words) to all employees about our new TurboTax Live feature launching next Tuesday. The tone should be energetic but professional. Include: what the feature does, why it matters to customers, and a call to action for employees to share on LinkedIn. Sign it from "The Product Team."

Iterating with Claude

You don't have to get the perfect prompt on the first try. Claude conversations are cumulative — you can refine as you go.

1

Start with a first draft prompt

Ask Claude to tackle your task. Even an imperfect prompt will get you a useful starting point.

2

Review and give feedback

Read Claude's response. What do you like? What's missing? Tell Claude directly: "Make it shorter" or "Add a section on risks."

3

Refine until it's right

Keep iterating. Most great outputs come from 2–4 rounds of back-and-forth, not one perfect prompt.

Understanding Claude's Limitations

⏰ Knowledge Cutoff

Claude's training data has a cutoff date. For current events or real-time data, verify with other sources.

🔢 Math & Logic

Claude is good at explaining math, but can make calculation errors. Always verify numbers independently.

🤔 Hallucinations

Claude may occasionally state things confidently that aren't accurate. Fact-check important claims.

💡 Golden Rule: Claude is your co-pilot, not autopilot. Always review and take responsibility for the final output, especially for customer-facing or high-stakes content.

Hands-On Activities

The best way to learn Claude is to use it. Try these real-world Intuit scenarios — copy the prompts and test them yourself in Claude.ai.

⏱ ~12 minutes
🎯 5 activities

Each activity below gives you a realistic Intuit work scenario and a ready-to-use prompt. Click Copy to grab the prompt, then open Claude.ai and try it out.

1
Summarize a Long Document
✍️ Writing · ⏱ 3 min · 👤 All employees
Scenario: Your manager just sent you a 40-page PDF of the Q4 strategic plan and wants you to share the key highlights with your team in a quick standup tomorrow.
Prompt to Try
I'm going to paste a long strategic planning document below. Please read it carefully and give me: 1. A 3-sentence executive summary 2. The top 5 priorities for next quarter (as bullets) 3. Any risks or concerns mentioned 4. Key dates or deadlines I should know about Keep the language simple — I need to explain this to my team in 5 minutes. [Paste your document here]
What to notice: Claude structures the output exactly as asked. Try changing the format (e.g., ask for a table instead of bullets) and see how Claude adapts.
2
Write a Professional Email
✍️ Communication · ⏱ 3 min · 👤 All employees
Scenario: A cross-functional project is running 2 weeks behind due to a dependency from another team. You need to send a diplomatic email to the team lead asking them to reprioritize without damaging the relationship.
Prompt to Try
Help me write a professional email to a peer team lead at Intuit. The context: - Our shared project (customer data pipeline) is 2 weeks behind schedule - Their team owns a key API integration that's blocking us - I want to escalate without creating conflict — we need this relationship long-term - Tone: direct but collaborative, not blaming The email should be under 200 words and end with a clear proposed next step (a 30-min sync this week).
What to notice: Notice how Claude balances directness with diplomacy. Try asking it to make the tone "more urgent" or "more casual" and compare the outputs.
3
Brainstorm Product Ideas
💡 Ideation · ⏱ 4 min · 👤 Product, Design, Engineering
Scenario: Your team is in early discovery for a new QuickBooks feature to help small business owners better understand their cash flow. You've been asked to come to the brainstorm with 10 ideas.
Prompt to Try
You are a senior product designer at a fintech company with deep experience in small business software. Generate 10 creative feature ideas for a QuickBooks cash flow dashboard aimed at small business owners (1–20 employees) who are not financial experts. For each idea, give: - Feature name (catchy, 2–4 words) - One sentence description - The core problem it solves Prioritize ideas that are simple to understand, visually engaging, and can be built incrementally. Think outside standard fintech — what would delight a small business owner who dreads looking at their finances?
What to notice: The "role" framing ("You are a senior product designer...") shapes Claude's perspective. Try removing it and see how the ideas change.
4
Explain a Technical Concept
🎓 Learning · ⏱ 3 min · 👤 All employees
Scenario: You keep hearing about "RAG" (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) in AI meetings but you're not sure what it means. You want a clear, jargon-free explanation.
Prompt to Try
Explain "Retrieval-Augmented Generation" (RAG) to me as if I'm a non-technical Intuit employee — someone who works in finance or operations and has a general understanding of AI but no engineering background. Use an analogy to make it concrete. Then explain: 1. Why it matters for enterprise AI 2. How Intuit might use it (one example) 3. One limitation I should know about Keep the whole response under 300 words.
What to notice: The "explain to me as if I'm..." framing controls complexity. Try changing the audience to "a 10-year-old" or "a software engineer" and compare.
5
Write an Excel Formula
📊 Data · ⏱ 3 min · 👤 Finance, Ops, Analytics
Scenario: You have a sales tracking spreadsheet with columns for Region, Product, and Revenue. You want a formula that sums revenue only for a specific region and product combination.
Prompt to Try
I have an Excel spreadsheet with these columns: - Column A: Region (e.g., "West", "East", "Central") - Column B: Product (e.g., "TurboTax", "QuickBooks", "Mint") - Column C: Revenue (numbers) Data starts at row 2. I want a formula that sums all revenue in Column C where Region = "West" AND Product = "TurboTax". Please: 1. Give me the exact formula 2. Explain how it works in plain English 3. Tell me how to modify it if I want to make the Region or Product dynamic (based on a dropdown cell)
What to notice: Claude gives you the formula AND explains the logic. Always ask Claude to explain — that's how you learn, not just copy-paste.
Module 5

Best Practices

Get the most out of Claude — safely, effectively, and responsibly. These habits separate power users from casual users.

⏱ ~7 minutes
⭐ 4 principles

Do's and Don'ts

✅ Do

  • Give Claude clear context and role framing
  • Specify the format you want (bullets, table, prose)
  • Iterate — ask for revisions and improvements
  • Fact-check important claims and numbers
  • Use Claude as a thought partner, not a replacement for judgment
  • Ask Claude to explain its reasoning ("Why did you suggest X?")
  • Use Projects in Claude.ai to give persistent context

❌ Don't

  • Paste confidential customer PII without checking policy
  • Treat Claude's output as fact without reviewing
  • Use Claude for decisions it shouldn't make alone (legal, medical)
  • Send sensitive competitive data to external Claude tools
  • Assume Claude knows Intuit-specific context it wasn't given
  • Submit Claude-generated work without a human review pass

Prompting Power Tips

🪄 Use "Think Step by Step"

For complex problems, add "Think through this step by step before answering." This dramatically improves accuracy on reasoning tasks.

📏 Specify Length

Don't accept a novel when you need a paragraph. Always tell Claude: "in under 100 words," "3 bullet points max," or "a full detailed outline."

🔄 Ask for Alternatives

Don't settle for the first version. Ask: "Give me 3 different versions of this" or "What would a more conservative version look like?"

🪞 Ask Claude to Review Its Own Work

After Claude gives an answer, try: "What are the weaknesses in this response?" or "What did I miss?" Claude is good at self-critique.

📎 Attach Context Files

Upload PDFs, spreadsheets, or text files directly. Claude can read and reason about long documents — use this instead of manually pasting.

💾 Use Projects for Ongoing Work

Create a Claude "Project" for recurring tasks (e.g., weekly reports). Add context once, and Claude remembers it across conversations.

Data Privacy at Intuit

⚠️ Data Handling: Before sharing any document or data with Claude, ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if this was shared outside Intuit?" If not, don't include it. Refer to Intuit's AI Use Policy for specific guidance on data classifications.
